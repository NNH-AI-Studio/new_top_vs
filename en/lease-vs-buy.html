Monthly Costs & Payment Structure

Leasing Payment Structure

  • Lower monthly payments: Typically 30-60% less than loan payments
  • Depreciation coverage: Only paying for vehicle's depreciation
  • Tax advantages: Business lease deductions possible
  • Down payment: $2,000-5,000 vs 10-20% for loans
  • Disposition fee: $300-500 at lease end

Buying Payment Structure

  • Higher monthly payments: Paying for full vehicle cost
  • Interest costs: Finance charges over loan term
  • Equity building: Building ownership stake
  • Flexible terms: 24-84 month loan options
  • No mileage restrictions: Drive as much as needed

Monthly Payment Comparison Example

$35,000 Vehicle Example:

Leasing (36 months): $400-500/month + $3,000 down

Buying (60 months): $600-700/month + $7,000 down

Monthly savings: $200-300 with leasing

Flexibility & Lifestyle Considerations

Leasing Flexibility Benefits

  • Drive newer cars: Change vehicles every 2-3 years
  • Lower commitment: Shorter contract terms
  • Warranty coverage: Usually covered under factory warranty
  • Technology access: Latest features and safety tech
  • Predictable costs: Fixed monthly payments

Buying Ownership Benefits

  • No restrictions: Unlimited mileage and modifications
  • Long-term value: Potential equity and resale value
  • Customization: Modify and personalize your vehicle
  • Family inheritance: Pass vehicle to family members
  • Selling flexibility: Sell or trade when convenient

Mileage Limits & Usage Considerations

Leasing Mileage Restrictions

  • Standard allowance: 10,000-15,000 miles per year
  • Overage fees: $0.15-0.35 per excess mile
  • Low mileage discounts: Reduced payments for lower usage
  • Business leasing: Higher mileage allowances available
  • Lease extensions: Option to extend if needed

Buying Mileage Freedom

  • Unlimited driving: No mileage restrictions or fees
  • High-mileage suitability: Perfect for frequent drivers
  • Commercial use: Ideal for business and delivery vehicles
  • Road trips: No concerns about excess mileage charges
  • Long-term planning: Keep vehicle as long as desired

End-of-Term Options & Disposition

Leasing End-of-Term Options

  • Return vehicle: Simply return to dealer
  • Purchase option: Buy vehicle at predetermined price
  • Lease transfer: Transfer lease to another person
  • Early termination: Possible with termination fees
  • New lease: Start new lease with different vehicle

Buying Ownership Options

  • Keep vehicle: Continue driving without restrictions
  • Sell privately: Potentially get more than trade-in value
  • Trade-in: Use as down payment for new vehicle
  • Refinance: Adjust loan terms if needed
  • Donate or scrap: Various disposition options

Tax Benefits & Business Use

Leasing Tax Advantages

  • Business deductions: 100% deductible for business use
  • Sales tax savings: Tax on payments vs full vehicle price
  • Section 179: Potential bonus depreciation
  • Write-offs: Lease payments may be tax deductible
  • GST/HST input credits: Recoverable sales tax

Buying Tax Advantages

  • Section 179 deduction: Deduct vehicle cost up to limits
  • Bonus depreciation: First-year depreciation benefits
  • Interest deduction: Loan interest may be deductible
  • Property tax: Potential property tax deductions
  • Capital gains: No capital gains on personal vehicles

Final Verdict

Choose Leasing if:

  • You want lower monthly payments
  • You drive fewer than 15,000 miles per year
  • You like driving newer cars every few years
  • You want to avoid major repair costs
  • You need a vehicle for business with tax benefits

Choose Buying if:

  • You drive more than 15,000 miles per year
  • You want to build equity in your vehicle
  • You prefer no mileage restrictions
  • You want to customize or modify your vehicle
  • You plan to keep the car long-term

Bottom Line: Leasing offers lower monthly payments, warranty coverage, and the ability to drive newer vehicles, making it ideal for those who want flexibility and lower upfront costs. Buying provides ownership, unlimited mileage, and long-term value, making it perfect for high-mileage drivers and those who want to build equity. Consider your driving habits, budget, and long-term vehicle needs when choosing between leasing and buying.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is cheaper in the long run?

Buying is typically cheaper long-term if you keep the vehicle for 5+ years, while leasing can be more expensive if you lease continuously due to never building equity.

Can I negotiate lease terms?

Yes, you can negotiate lease terms including monthly payments, mileage allowances, and acquisition fees, similar to negotiating a car purchase.

What happens if I exceed mileage limits?

You'll pay excess mileage fees ($0.15-0.35 per mile) at lease end, which can add up significantly for high-mileage drivers.

Can I buy my leased car?

Yes, most leases include a purchase option allowing you to buy the vehicle at a predetermined price at lease end.
